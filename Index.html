Quaid-e-Azam






Stanley Wolpert in his book Jinnah of Pakistan writes “Few individuals significantly alter the course of history. Fewer still modify the map of the world. Hardly anyone can be credited with creating a nation-state. Mohammad Ali Jinnah did all three.”

Muhammad Ali Jinnah aka Quaid-e-Azam was born on 25 December 1876 and died on 11 September 1948. He was the founder of Pakistan. He worked as the leader of the All-India Muslim League from 1913. Up to, the establishment of Pakistan, which happened on 14 August 1947. Later, as the Dominion of Pakistan’s first governor-general until his death.

Mainly life of quaid e azam can be divided into 3 stages:

  • Early Life and Education
  • Legal Career
  • Political Career
  • Role in the Creation of Pakistan
  • Legacy

    • Early life and Education:

    Muhammad Ali Jinnah was born on December 25, 1876, in Karachi, which was then a part of British India. He was the eldest of seven children born to his parents, who were Ismaili Muslims from the Kathiawar region of present-day Gujarat, India

    Jinnah's father, Jinnahbhai Poonja, was a successful merchant and Jinnah received his early education in Karachi. In 1887, at the age of 11, Jinnah traveled to Bombay to continue his education at the Christian Missionary Society's high school. He later attended the Sindh-Madrasa-tul-Islam and the Bombay University, where he studied law.

    “Think a hundred times before you take a decision, but once that decision is taken, stand by it as one man.” ~Quaid-e-Azam~

    Legal Career:

    After completing his education, Jinnah started his legal practice in Bombay in 1896. He quickly became known as a brilliant lawyer and was recognized for his expertise in constitutional law. He also served as the President of the All India Muslim League from 1913 to 1914.

    Political Career:

    Jinnah became increasingly involved in politics and joined the Indian National Congress in 1906 , which was then fighting for India's independence from British rule. However, over time, he became disillusioned with the Congress and the Hindu-dominated leadership, which he felt was neglecting the interests of the Muslim community. This led him to eventually break away from the Congress and become a leading figure in the movement for a separate Muslim state, which ultimately resulted in the creation of Pakistan in 1947.

    “With faith, discipline and selfless devotion to duty, there is nothing worthwhile that you cannot achieve.” ~Quaid-e-Azam~

    Role in Creation of Pakistan:

    Jinnah is widely regarded as the father of Pakistan, as he was the primary driving force behind the creation of the country. He worked tirelessly for the cause of a separate Muslim state, and his vision and leadership played a crucial role in the achievement of that goal.

    Jinnah served as the first Governor-General of Pakistan from 1947 until his death in 1948. He died on September 11, 1948, in Karachi, Pakistan, at the age of 71.

    Legacy:

    Jinnah is remembered as one of the most important figures in the history of Pakistan and South Asia. His leadership, vision, and unwavering commitment to the cause of Pakistan continue to inspire people around the world today. Jinnah's birthday, December 25, is celebrated as Quaid-e-Azam Day in Pakistan, in honor of his contributions to the country.

    “There is no power on earth that can undo Pakistan.” ~Quaid-e-Azam~
